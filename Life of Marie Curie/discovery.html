Scientific discovery

Henri Becquerel was studying the effects of light on uranium salts. He placed uranium salts on a photographic plate wrapped in black paper. Despite the lack of sunlight due to overcast weather, he uncovered an image of a copper Maltes on top of the plate. This showed that uranium salts were emitting radiation, in which Marie Curie was intrigued by.

Using that experiment as a thesis, she began work with tons of pitch blend to find the new element. Marie for many years would try to separate solids from the element and the pitch blend. She would mix acids with the pitch blend and through filtering, separate the solids and the element. It took 4 years for her to hone in it.

CHemical analysis confirmed that they discovered a new element. This ended up being one of the most dangerous, which they ended up calling radium. This discovery got used for energy production, scientific research, and even big projects like them Manhattan project. Pierre Curie put radium on his arm and discovered that radium was strong enough to kill cancer cells. This lead to a breakthrough of medicine that makes cancer treatable.
