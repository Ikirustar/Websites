Play With Us

Swim Team

The Adaptive Sports New England swim team meets weekly and brings together competitive swimmers from across the region. Many team members also participate on a club or school swim program. The team is coached by Paralympic medalists Mike Prout and Anna Johannes who design specific programs for each athlete, focusing on technical skills and mental preparation. Each swimmer receives an individualized program to help work on their technical and psychological skills. This program is designed for intermediate and advanced swimmers looking to improve their swimming stroke and fitness and takes place weekly at Simmons University. As with all of our programs, this for athletes with a visual or mobility impairment.

Swim Lessons

The swim lesson program is offered in partnership with local YMCAs. Lessons are staffed by YMCA professionals with oversight provided by Mike Prout and Mary Gawron. Mike is an experienced swim teacher and Paralympic medalist, while Mary has over 25 years swim instruction experience. Lessons are generally taught at a 1 to 1 student to teacher ratio. Adaptive Sports New England’s swim lesson program is held in partnership with the South Shore YMCA. The lessons are overseen by Paralympian Michael Prout, while the lessons are taught by YMCA professionals. These lessons are great for children through their one on one student to teacher ratio, where swimming skills can be developed and improved.

As with all Adaptive Sports New England programs, this program is specifically designed for those with visual or mobility impairments. Participating children should demonstrate typical cognitive and behavioral development. We expect this program to fill up. If you would like to express interest or reserve a spot, please fill out the interest form above. For ages 6 – 18.

Rowing with Community Rowing, Inc.

Community Rowing in Brighton offers a number of Para rowing programs including open Para rowing, a high performance Para rowing training group and a teen Para rowing program run in partnership with Adaptive Sports New England. Outdoor programs feature training on the Charles River, while the winter season is held indoors at the Community Rowing facility.

Adaptive Sports New England’s rowing program is one of the para rowing programs that are offered by Community Rowing Inc. out of their boathouse in Brighton on the Charles River. This Teen para rowing program is available throughout most of the year with the warmer months spent out on the water in varying boats and the winter season being held indoors at the Community Rowing facility. This is available for both athletes with a mobility impairment or visual impairment and is always a great time learning to row. This program is coached by staff at Community Rowing and many volunteers with rowing experience as well who join the athletes within smaller sculling boats. For ages 12 – 19.

Track

The Adaptive Sports New England track program meets weekly with skilled coaches to improve racing techniques. Currently, wheelchair track is being offered at Regis College on Saturday mornings. Track accommodates athletes with visual impairments, Cerebral Palsy, spina Bifida, spinal cord injury, amputations, dwarfism and various other physical impairments.

Adaptive Sports New England’s track program is held on a weekly basis with experienced coaches who can aid athletes in improving their running technique and racing technique. Our track program is available for athletes with a lower body impairment who have good use of their arms.

Wheelchair Basketball

The wheelchair basketball program meets once per week and brings together youth and young adults for drills, pick-up games and fun! Participants must have good use of their arms and upper bodies to fully enjoy this sport. Players include youth affected by Spina Bifida, spinal cord injury, Cerebral Palsy, leg amputations and other impairments.

Adaptive Sports New England and Franciscan Children’s wheelchair basketball program occurs weekly for youth ages 5 – 21 and brings athletes together to learn the sport of wheelchair basketball. This program runs during the fall and winter and provides the opportunity for athletes to participate in both drills and fun pick-up basketball games. In order to fully enjoy and participate, all athletes must have good use of their arms and upper bodies.

Sailing

Adaptive Sports New England partners with Boston’s Community Boating on the Charles River Esplanade to teach youth who have a visual or mobility impairment to sail. Youth focus on building confidence and strengthening their communication and teamwork skills while simultaneously becoming better sailors.

This program is run in partnership with Community Boating in Boston and takes place on the Charles River near the Esplanade. In this program, emphasis is placed on sailing skills in addition to developing an athlete’s confidence, communication, and teamwork. This program is typically offered weekly during the summer months for youth ages 7- 18. For participants with a visual or mobility impairment

Beep Baseball

The beep baseball program is designed for those with a visual impairment, and is fun for all ages. Balls and bases emit sounds so that players can compete without the use of their vision. Clinics are held weekly with Coach Joe Quintanilla.

Adaptive Sports New England’s new beep baseball program, the Boston Strong, is an amazing opportunity for individuals with a visual impairment to learn and play the incredible sport of beep baseball. This program is available for both youth and adults, and is certainly fun for everyone. The sport is an adaptive form of baseball. The balls and bases emit beeping and buzzing sounds respectively so that players can locate them. Our team is a part of the National Beep Baseball Association, and will travel and play in tournaments- but our weekly practices are welcome for anyone to attend who want to get exercise, learn a new skill, build confidence and inner strength. No previous experience is needed, just a desire to learn and have fun while being active. This program is located at Tufts University on Sunday mornings with the practices and team being ran by Head Coach Joe Quintanilla. Junior Varsity Series

The programs in the Junior Varsity series are designed for children ages 5-10. Events are held multiple times per year, and will engage youths in adaptive sports stations and games. This series is designed to introduce younger kids to the para versions of the sports they love and to new activities all together.

Adaptive Sports New England’s Junior Varsity Sports Series is a way for younger kids to be introduced to adaptive sports that are available. This series is offered for children ages 5-10 with visual or mobility impairments and their families with events being held multiple times throughout the year. It is a great way to get these younger kids exposed to para sports and to connect with other young athletes their age. This program is a wonderful starting point for these young kids to see if they want to continue with some of the other programs that Adaptive Sports New England offers.
